Profit Hub Structure Pivots

Swing points mark pivots.

A swing high or low is formed around candles and gives structure a reference point.

03

Profit Hub PD Arrays Playbook

PD Arrays are reaction zones.

They show where price may respond after displacement or while liquidity is being delivered. The zone creates location; context decides whether it deserves an entry.

13
PD Arrays Include
  • Fair Value Gaps (FVG)
  • Inversion Fair Value Gaps (iFVG)
  • Order Blocks
  • Breaker Blocks
PROFIT HUB LENSDo not treat every zone as an automatic entry.
CONTEXT FIRSTPD Arrays work best when they agree with daily bias and the draw on liquidity.
Fair Value Gaps
Profit Hub Three-Candle Imbalance

Fair Value Gaps form when price moves aggressively.

An FVG is a three-candle imbalance. Candle two creates the displacement, leaving a gap between candle one’s wick and candle three’s wick.

14
The middle candle creates the imbalance.
Mark candle one wick to candle three wick.
Price may return to rebalance the gap.
Use the gap as a reaction or entry area only after confirmation.
The imbalance is the zone
ENTRY RULEThe entry comes only after your model confirms the zone.
Profit Hub Bullish FVG Examples

Reaction from the gap, continuation higher.

The clean bullish sequence is bias higher, displacement, retracement into the imbalance, and a reaction that confirms the zone.

15
Context

Bias favors higher prices and displacement leaves a clear imbalance.

Execution

Price retraces into the FVG and the reaction confirms the entry.

Risk / Target

Stop below the model. Target buy-side or opposing liquidity.

BEST VERSIONA bullish FVG is strongest when it forms after clean displacement.
Profit Hub Bearish FVG Example

A bearish FVG can act as resistance.

When bias favors lower prices, wait for downside displacement and a retracement back into the imbalance before looking for the sell model.

16
  • Bias favors lower prices.
  • Price creates downside displacement.
  • The retracement returns to the FVG.
  • The entry forms near the gap edge.
  • Target sell-side or opposing liquidity.
Sell model • invalidation above
FLIP CONDITIONIf price trades through the zone and closes past it, think iFVG next.
Profit Hub How to Trade FVGs

Use the gap for location, then measure the trade.

The zone alone is not the trade. Build the idea from liquidity, displacement, invalidation, and the opposing target.

17
Find the draw on liquidity.
Wait for displacement.
Mark the FVG.
Enter at the top or bottom of the zone.
Place SL beyond invalidation.
Target opposing liquidity.
PROTECT CAPITALIf the zone gives no reaction, do not marry the idea.

Inversion Fair Value Gaps
Profit Hub Inversion FVG

An iFVG is an FVG that gets disrespected.

A candle body closes through the original FVG on the same timeframe, the zone flips, and the retest can create an entry toward opposing liquidity.

19
SAME-TIMEFRAME RULEThe confirming close must occur on the timeframe the original FVG was drawn on.
Profit Hub Bullish iFVG Trade

Let the inverted zone prove itself on the retest.

Once price respects the flipped zone, the next opposing liquidity pool becomes the target and risk stays below invalidation.

20
  • The original FVG gets invalidated.
  • The zone becomes an iFVG.
  • The retest creates the entry area.
  • Target the next opposing liquidity.
  • Keep risk below invalidation.
BEST VERSIONClear displacement through the zone before the retest.
Profit Hub Bearish iFVG Sequence

The same concept works in reverse.

A bullish FVG below price gets closed through, flips bearish, and becomes a potential sell zone only after price comes back to retest it.

21
DO NOT FRONT-RUNLet price come back into the zone before looking for the entry.
Profit Hub Bearish iFVG Trade Example

Trade the reaction, not the hope.

Retest the inverted zone, enter with confirmation, place the stop beyond invalidation, and target sell-side liquidity.

22
NO REJECTION = NO TRADEIf the retest does not reject, there is no reason to force the setup.
