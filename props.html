What is a prop firm?

A prop firm gives traders a path to qualify for a funded account without needing to put a large personal trading balance on the line.

🏦

How the process works

You pay for an evaluation and trade under a defined set of rules. After meeting the profit target without violating the drawdown or consistency requirements, you can qualify for a funded account and become eligible for payouts under the firm's terms.

01 Buy an evaluation Choose an account size and learn every rule before placing a trade.
02 Prove consistency Reach the target while respecting the firm's drawdown and risk limits.
03 Earn payouts Keep following the funded-account rules and request payouts when eligible.

Why many beginners start with a prop firm

The main advantage is controlled upfront risk. Instead of depositing thousands into a personal futures account, your immediate financial risk is generally limited to evaluation, reset, and activation fees.

💵
Less personal capital exposed A failed evaluation costs the fee, rather than a large personal trading balance.
🛡️
Built-in risk limits Drawdown rules, daily limits, and platform controls can force better discipline.
📈
A clearer path to scale Consistent traders can access larger account sizes without first saving the full balance themselves.
Important: Prop firms are not free money. Strict rules, recurring fees, payout conditions, and firm risk still matter. The smarter approach is to use one account, trade small, and treat the evaluation fee as money you can afford to lose.

Scaling guide

Earn the right to scale.

Scaling should be the result of proven consistency—not excitement, impatience, or a desire to make more money faster.

↗

Begin with one account

Focus on getting one account funded, then take two or three payouts from it. Before adding more accounts, prove that you can protect the account and repeat your process under funded-account pressure.

Your first scaling checkpoint
2–3 payouts

See whether you can complete this within one month. If you can, you may be ready to begin scaling carefully.

01

Add one evaluation—not max allocation

Purchase one additional evaluation at the same prop firm. This lets you use the firm's built-in free copy trader instead of immediately managing accounts across multiple platforms.

02

Make the evaluation a trailing account

Keep trading your funded account exactly as you already were and let the evaluation follow it. You are not trying to force a pass—the evaluation should pass naturally as your normal, disciplined trading continues.

03

Stagger each new evaluation

Buy one evaluation, wait one to two trading days, and then purchase the next. This reduces open exposure because an evaluation you have not purchased yet cannot be affected by a losing day on your current accounts.

04

Build to three funded accounts first

Once you have three funded accounts, pause the expansion and focus on earning a payout from all three. After proving you can manage them together, repeat the same staggered process for the remaining two accounts.

05

Respect every evaluation

Never trade carelessly because it is “just an evaluation.” You cannot say your rules matter while ignoring them when the account feels less valuable. These accounts can change your life—but only when you respect the account, respect yourself, and respect your rules.

This is not a get-rich-quick scheme.

With patience and discipline, the effects compound and you can see a dramatic increase in income over time. Stay focused on taking excellent trades, following your process, and protecting the accounts. Do not trade while thinking about the money.
