01 / Definition

What is SMT divergence?

Smart Money Technique

A type of manipulation revealed when NQ and ES disagree at corresponding highs or lows.

In plain English

One market takes liquidity while the other refuses to confirm the move. That mismatch can reveal that the push was manipulation rather than genuine strength or weakness.

02 / Direction

Bearish at the highs. Bullish at the lows.

↗

Bearish SMT

Bearish SMT forms on the highs. NQ or ES makes a higher high while the other fails to confirm it, exposing potential buy-side manipulation.

↘

Bullish SMT

Bullish SMT forms on the lows. NQ or ES makes a lower low while the other holds its corresponding low, exposing potential sell-side manipulation.

05 / Timeframes

More candles apart can reveal a bigger SMT.

Higher-timeframe SMT
Further apart.

On a one-minute chart, higher-timeframe SMTs appear farther apart and have more candles between their two comparison points. They tend to be more powerful.

Smaller internal SMT
Closer together.

Internal SMTs span fewer one-minute candles. They can still add confluence, but usually carry less weight than a properly aligned higher-timeframe SMT.

!

Power does not replace alignment. Higher-timeframe SMT must still agree with the general higher-timeframe narrative, bias, objective and draw on liquidity.

06 / Non-negotiables

Confluence—not a trade signal.

Never pattern trade. Never enter just because an SMT forms.

01

Compare only NQ and ES

Use corresponding highs or lows from the same market window. Do not force unrelated swing points together.

02

Identify the manipulation

One market takes liquidity while its correlated partner refuses to confirm the move.

03

Align the higher-timeframe narrative

The SMT should agree with your general bias, objective and draw on liquidity.

04

Wait for confirmation

Use displacement and your execution model. The divergence alone is not permission to enter.

05

Use it as added confluence

SMT strengthens a complete trade idea; it never creates a complete trade idea by itself.
