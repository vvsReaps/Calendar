Turtle Soup

Price takes its draw, builds a trap, stops out the crowd—and then delivers the real move.

This is not a pattern to trade in isolation. Narrative, higher-timeframe bias and a clear liquidity objective come first.

01 / Context first

The Turtle Model begins with a draw.

The non-negotiable filter

Do not pattern trade. Narrative + HTF bias + an objective in liquidity must align.

Where to look

Usually at an external or higher-timeframe draw. If it forms at an internal draw, mark it out—and pay close attention.

02 / Turtle Model

How the trap is constructed.

01

Take the draw

Price first reaches the external, higher-timeframe or clearly defined liquidity objective you were anticipating.

02

Leave a high or low

A clean swing is left behind. That obvious level becomes a natural place for the crowd to protect its new position.

03

Fake the move

Price moves away from the draw with enough conviction to FOMO the 95% into the apparent reversal.

04

Return and soup

Price reverses with speed and efficiency back toward the obvious high or low that was left behind.

05

Stop them out

The level is swept, forcing traders out of the position they chased during the fake move.

06

Deliver the real move

With the trap cleared, price delivers the actual reversal—back down in the bearish example shown.

“I like seeing the 95% get stopped out before I take my trade.”
The core patience principle
03 / Execution

Read the move. Don’t become it.

01

Do not FOMO into the fake move.

That first burst away from the draw is the bait. Observe who is being invited into the market.

02

Wait for the stop-out.

The turtle soup is the event. Let price return to and sweep the obvious high or low before expecting delivery.

03

Do not trade against the narrative.

The model has meaning only when the location, higher-timeframe bias and liquidity objective agree.

04

Use SMT as supporting evidence.

If ES takes a high while NQ does not, that divergence can strengthen a bearish read—but it never replaces context.

The Turtle Model is the entry framework. Narrative and the draw decide whether the sequence should work at all.

04 / SMT confluence

One pair raids. The divergence speaks.

The Turtle Model plays out. Only one pair soups.

What happened

Both correlated markets form the Turtle Model, but only one pair performs the Turtle Soup / stop raid. That mismatch creates bearish SMT.

Why it is valid

The higher-timeframe narrative is bearish and the objective is to seek shorts. When bearish SMT forms and begins to deliver, it can be used as additional confluence.

Wait for candle closure and true displacement in the direction of the HTF narrative and draw.
05 / When it fails

A failed reversal can become continuation.

Bullish narrative
→
Draw remains above

Location changes the meaning.

If the reversal model forms at an internal draw while the higher-timeframe narrative remains bullish, a full reversal would contradict the active objective above.

Anticipate that the reversal model may fail. Its failure can then provide an entry framework for continuation toward the original draw above.
