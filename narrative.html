Foundation lesson / AMD

Power of 3: how price delivers.

Watch directly on Loom if the video does not load ↗
A

Accumulation

Price prints through time without making meaningful progress up or down. It moves sideways, taking relative highs and lows while both long and short orders build inside the range.

M

Manipulation

Price breaks from the range in a misleading direction. The move encourages traders to commit early and places their stops where the market can use that liquidity.

D

Delivery

Price expands toward the real objective with displacement and directional structure. This is the phase where the market is moving productively and where trades should be sought.

Do not trade the print. Trade the delivery.
Accumulation exists to build orders, not to offer clean direction.
  • Avoid repeatedly buying highs and selling lows inside the range.
  • Do not chase the manipulation break.
  • Wait for strong displacement and an actual uptrend or downtrend.
Chart application

Read the full sequence.

Mark the sideways order-building range, identify the false move beyond it, then require expansion candles in the true direction before considering an entry.

Supporting confluence

SMT can expose manipulation.

In the lesson example, NQ takes the draw while ES does not, forming SMT during manipulation before price begins delivering to the downside.

Module 03

Identify Low Probability Days.

03

Low probability can often be identified before New York opens by asking what liquidity has already been taken, how much of the average daily range has already been used, and whether price is still delivering with meaningful displacement.

Watch directly on Loom if the video does not load ↗
01 / Liquidity

Major draws are already taken.

Previous-day high and low are often important pools of buy-side and sell-side liquidity. In the lesson, Asia takes the previous-day low, expands roughly 500 points, and then takes the previous-day high before New York opens.

02 / Range

ADR is already exhausted.

In this specific example, NQ had moved about 1.89% while its displayed average daily range was roughly 1.84%. Much of the expected 23-hour session move had already occurred.

03 / Structure

Zoom out and check progress.

One-minute candles may appear active up close, but the wider view shows New York moving sideways instead of making productive progress toward a remaining draw.

04 / Candles

Measure displacement.

The session begins with a roughly 40-point candle, then contracts into approximately 18, 14, 13, and 16-point candles. The action becomes small, overlapping, and wicky instead of consistently body-heavy.

Think of the market like an old dog.

It can run, play, and cover distance, but once the important objectives and normal range are completed, it needs to rest and rebuild accumulation before beginning another productive cycle.

If major draws are cleared, ADR is spent, and candles are shrinking into wicky sideways action, do not force a New York trade.
Higher-probability contrast

Look for unfinished business.

A better environment may have limited Asia movement, remaining liquidity draws, room inside ADR, a clear HTF narrative, and consistent 30 to 40-plus point body-heavy candles.

Important exception

Context can keep the day alive.

If price has rebuilt accumulation, important draws still remain, and the higher-timeframe narrative is intact, the session can still produce a valid move. ADR is context, not a standalone signal.

Module 04

Everything in One Trading Day.

04

This session shows that a correct narrative does not always create a clean trade. Power of 3 forms across Asia, London, premarket, and New York, but the final expansion is too wicky and inefficient to offer the displacement required for a high-probability entry.

Watch directly on Loom if the video does not load ↗
Asia and London

Asia generates liquidity and trades toward a low. London continues chopping, engineering additional highs and lows without establishing clean direction.

Premarket accumulation

From roughly 5:00 to 6:00 a.m., price remains inside a range and continues building liquidity for the later expansion.

Manipulation before the open

Near 9:00 a.m., price raids a nearby low. This completes the manipulation phase before the opening bell rather than waiting for New York to create another false move.

New York expansion

With the bullish narrative and downside manipulation already established, New York expands upward and takes the remaining liquidity draws.

Correct idea, poor entry

The market spends roughly ten minutes chopping, prints wicky candles, lacks strong displacement, and fails to build a clean higher-high and higher-low trend. The narrative works, but the trade quality remains low.

Do not demand a second manipulation

Many traders automatically wait for manipulation at market open. If manipulation already occurred in premarket, New York may move directly into expansion.

What true delivery should show

A higher-probability bullish move should print large body-heavy candles, move with speed, and continue forming clear higher highs and higher lows.

Build the memory bank

Compare the premarket call with what actually prints. Learning how the sessions connect builds pattern recognition for what New York is likely to do without forcing an entry.
